**Thinning the Gibbs sampler?**

The most difficult parameter to converge is $\delta^2$. We look at the auto correlation coefficient(sample) of the iterates.

===CLAIMS===

CLAIM c1: The stated $\delta^2 = (\ell-1)^{-1}\sum_{i=1}^{\ell}(\hat\nu_i-\bar\nu)^2$ with $\bar\nu=\ell^{-1}\sum\hat\nu_i$ is unbiased for the variance of the $\hat\nu_i$ (the $(\ell-1)^{-1}$ divisor, not $\ell^{-1}$, is the correct one).
```python
from sympy import *
from sympy.stats import Normal, E as Expect

# check E[delta^2] = sigma_nu^2 exactly, for several small ell, with iid nu_hat_i
mu, s = symbols('mu sigma', real=True, positive=True)

for ell in [2, 3, 4]:
    nus = [Normal('n%d' % i, mu, s) for i in range(ell)]
    nubar = Rational(1, ell) * sum(nus)
    delta2 = Rational(1, ell - 1) * sum((nu - nubar)**2 for nu in nus)
    # booklet's claim: this estimator is the (unbiased) variance of the nu_hat's
    assert simplify(Expect(delta2) - s**2) == 0, ell
    # and the naive 1/ell divisor would NOT be unbiased -> confirms (ell-1)^{-1} is decisive
    delta2_naive = Rational(1, ell) * sum((nu - nubar)**2 for nu in nus)
    assert simplify(Expect(delta2_naive) - s**2) != 0, ell

# also confirm the algebraic identity sum (nu_i - nubar)^2 = sum nu_i^2 - ell*nubar^2
ell = 5
x = symbols('x0:5', real=True)
xbar = Rational(1, ell) * sum(x)
assert simplify(sum((xi - xbar)**2 for xi in x) - (sum(xi**2 for xi in x) - ell * xbar**2)) == 0
```
