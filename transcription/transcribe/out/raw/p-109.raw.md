**10.2.5 Effective sample size**

Have $M$ iterates (length of chain under study).
Compute the auto correlations at the $i^{th}$ lag, $r_i$, $i = 1, 2, ...$
Find $k$ such that

$$|r_k| < \frac{2}{\sqrt{M}}$$

Compute

$$\tau = \frac{1}{2} + \sum_{i=1}^{k} r_i$$

The effective sample size is given

$$eff_{ss} = \frac{M}{2\tau}$$

> ⏳ VERIFY[c1]: $eff_{ss} = M/(2\tau)$ equals $M$ exactly when $\tau = 1/2$, and equals $0.5M$ when $\tau = 1$.

`Note:` Want $\tau \approx \frac{1}{2}$ and $eff_{ss} \approx M$. Of course, $\tau$ can differ from $\frac{1}{2}$ and $eff_{ss}$ can be less than $M$, say $eff_{ss}$ as small as $0.5M$ may be reasonable.

## 10.3 Rao-Blackwellized estimators

Suppose that we have a random sample $\{(U^{(i)}, V^{(i)}, W^{(i)}), i = 1, ..., M\}$ from the joint density $[U, V, W]$. Then, $U^{(1)}, U^{(2)}, ..., U^{(M)}$ are a random sample from the marginal distribution of $U$. Thus, an estimator of $E(U)$ is

$$\widehat{E(U)} = M^{-1} \sum_{i=1}^{M} U^{(i)}$$

and similarly, an estimator of $E(U^2)$ is

$$\widehat{E(U^2)} = M^{-1} \sum_{i=1}^{M} U^{(i)2}$$

Also $Var(U)$ can be estimated by

$$\widehat{Var(U)} = \widehat{E(U^2)} - (\widehat{E(U)})^2 \approx S^2 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{M}(U^{(i)} - \widehat{E(U)})^2}{M-1}$$

> ⏳ VERIFY[c2]: $M^{-1}\sum U_i^2 - (M^{-1}\sum U_i)^2 = \frac{1}{M}\sum (U_i - \bar U)^2 = \frac{M-1}{M} S^2$, so the stated $\approx$ (not $=$) is the correct relation.

In some situation, there may be better estimators of $E(U), Var(U)$ etc. An estimator with smaller mean square error is obtained by exploiting the form of the conditional distribution of $[U|V, W]$.
For

$$[U] = \int \int [U|V,W][V,W] dV\, dW,$$

$$\widehat{[U]} = M^{-1} \sum_{i=1}^{M} [U|V^{(i)}, W^{(i)}]$$

CLAIM c1: The effective sample size $eff_{ss}=M/(2\tau)$ equals $M$ at $\tau=1/2$ and $0.5M$ at $\tau=1$.
```python
from sympy import *

M, tau = symbols('M tau', positive=True)
eff = M / (2*tau)

# Want tau ~ 1/2  =>  eff_ss ~ M
assert simplify(eff.subs(tau, Rational(1, 2)) - M) == 0

# eff_ss as small as 0.5M corresponds to tau = 1
assert simplify(eff.subs(tau, 1) - M/2) == 0

# eff_ss is decreasing in tau (so tau > 1/2 => eff_ss < M)
assert simplify(diff(eff, tau)) == -M/(2*tau**2)
assert (simplify(diff(eff, tau)) < 0) == True
```

CLAIM c2: $\widehat{E(U^2)}-(\widehat{E(U)})^2$ equals $\frac{1}{M}\sum(U_i-\bar U)^2 = \frac{M-1}{M}S^2$, hence only approximately $S^2$.
```python
from sympy import *

# Do it exactly for a general M via a small symbolic sample, then confirm
# the M-dependence of the ratio for several M.
for m in range(2, 8):
    Us = symbols(f'U1:{m+1}', real=True)
    Ubar = Rational(1, m) * sum(Us)
    lhs = Rational(1, m) * sum(u**2 for u in Us) - Ubar**2      # E(U^2)hat - (E(U)hat)^2
    ssq = sum((u - Ubar)**2 for u in Us) / (m - 1)              # S^2 with M-1 divisor
    # exact identity: lhs = (m-1)/m * S^2
    assert simplify(lhs - Rational(m - 1, m) * ssq) == 0
    # and therefore lhs != S^2 in general (approximation only)
    assert simplify(lhs - ssq) != 0

# Equivalently, lhs is the 1/M-divisor variance
m = 5
Us = symbols(f'U1:{m+1}', real=True)
Ubar = Rational(1, m) * sum(Us)
lhs = Rational(1, m) * sum(u**2 for u in Us) - Ubar**2
assert simplify(lhs - sum((u - Ubar)**2 for u in Us) / m) == 0

# ratio -> 1 as M -> oo, justifying the printed "≈"
M = symbols('M', positive=True)
assert limit((M - 1)/M, M, oo) == 1
```
