As an example of an experiment in which the analysis of covariance may be employed,
consider a study performed to determine if there is a difference in the strength of a monol-
ament ber produced by three different machines. The data from this experiment are shown
in Table 15.10. Figure 15.3 presents a scatter diagram of strength (y) versus the diameter
(or thickness) of the sample. Clearly, the strength of the ber is also affected by its thickness;
consequently, a thicker ber will generally be stronger than a thinner one. The analysis of
covariance could be used to remove the effect of thickness (x) on strength (y) when testing for
differences in strength between machines.
