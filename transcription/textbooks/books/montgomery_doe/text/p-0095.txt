Simultaneous Confidence Intervals. The confidence interval expressions given
in Equations 3.12 and 3.13 are one-at-a-time confidence intervals. That is, the confidence
level 1 юААюАЗapplies to only one particular estimate. However, in many problems, the exper-
imenter may wish to calculate several confidence intervals, one for each of a number of
means or differences between means. If there are r such 100(1 юААюАЗ) percent confidence
intervals of interest, the probability that the r intervals will simultaneously be correct is at
least 1 юААrюАЗ. The probability rюАЗis often called the experimentwise error rate or overall
confidence coefficient. The number of intervals r does not have to be large before the set of
confidence intervals becomes relatively uninformative. For example, if there are r юАГ5
intervals and юАЗюАГ0.05 (a typical choice), the simultaneous confidence level for the set of
five confidence intervals is at least 0.75, and if r юАГ10 and юАЗюАГ0.05, the simultaneous con-
fidence level is at least 0.50.
One approach to ensuring that the simultaneous conюБйdence level is not too small is to
replace юАЗ/2 in the one-at-a-time conюБйdence interval Equations 3.12 and 3.13 with юАЗ/(2r). This
is called the Bonferroni method, and it allows the experimenter to construct a set of r simul-
taneous conюБйdence intervals on treatment means or differences in treatment means for which
the overall conюБйdence level is at least 100(1 юААюАЗ) percent. When r is not too large, this is a
very nice method that leads to reasonably short conюБйdence intervals. For more information,
refer to the supplemental text material for Chapter 3.
