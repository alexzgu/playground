## What is right (verified, not vibes)

- **Centerpiece delivers.** §06.5 is staged Setup → Predict ("Commit to a number... Hold onto that 50%") → Run → Reconcile; the bars figure makes the pathology *felt* — every green bar covers θ, orange bars visibly miss (inspected PNG); the r/(1−r)-then-1 curve is plotted with binned simulation dots landing on it, checkpoints `0.111/0.429/1.000` printed and quoted. Panel mandate met in full.
- **Ordering per panel mandate**: loss→estimator concrete material (06.1–06.3: trio, newsvendor, MAP/lasso) all precede interval philosophy (06.4–06.6). Verified.
- **Skeleton complete**; spine is one stateable sentence and the final Takeaway restates it as a reflex; 13 blocks (cap 4–16); all four figures referenced AND discussed in prose; all backtick numbers cross-checked against the log — only the Ex 06.3 `9` fails.
- **Non-tribal**: "The confidence interval is not wrong — 50% is the correct long-run frequency of the *procedure*"; Pitfall 3 calls the pre-data guarantee "a feature... not a defect"; Ex 06.2 Reconcile concedes CI validity while showing its scope. No gloating.
- **Exercises**: all five in strict §5 format; 06.1/06.2/06.4/06.5 genuinely surprising (100% conditional coverage; CI asserting impossible θ; mean 0.2410 vs mode 0); 06.3/06.4 hit the ML/ops bridge. The ET-vs-HPD replacement (06.5) *does* teach "a set is a decision" — it names the loss ("shorter is better"), shows equal-tailed excluding a point 3× more probable than points it includes, and closes with "there is no default that is free of a loss." As good as, arguably better than, a generic HPD derivation.
- **Fluff scan**: clean. No banned openers, no throat-clearing; the few flourishes ("biting hard", "earns its keep") are dry and load-bearing. Nothing quotable as an offender.

## Learner's-eye summary (5 lines)

I committed to "50%" on the R=0.9 interval and was cleanly caught — the bars picture then made it impossible to un-see, and the curve-plus-dots settled it; that experience alone is the module. The loss trio and newsvendor taught me the recipe but never asked me to guess first, so they read as (very good) lecture rather than the trap §06.5 sets. Exercise 06.3's "falls to the median 9" against the code's printed 10 made me distrust the module for five minutes until I re-derived it myself — exactly the trust the numbers contract exists to protect. The garbled formula in Ex 06.2's setup cost me a re-read before I gave up and used the code. A week later I can state the spine verbatim: the loss picks the summary; what you condition on picks the guarantee.
