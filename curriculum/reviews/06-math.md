## Adjudication of the author's deliberate deviation

**Claim.** The planned "two procedures on the two-point uniform" exercise is unsound because "large R makes the midrange concentrate near θ, so a mean-centered interval also becomes certain."

**Ruling: the author is RIGHT.** For $X_1,X_2 \sim U(\theta-\frac12, \theta+\frac12)$, conditional on $R=r$ the minimum $X_{(1)}$ is uniform on $[\theta-\frac12,\ \theta+\frac12-r]$, hence the midrange $M = X_{(1)} + r/2$ is uniform on $[\theta - \frac{1-r}{2},\ \theta + \frac{1-r}{2}]$ — an interval of width $1-r$ centered at θ. So $P(|M-\theta|\le c \mid R=r) = \min\!\big(\tfrac{2c}{1-r},\,1\big)$, which → 1 as $r \to 1$ for **any** fixed half-width $c$ (certain once $r \ge 1-2c$). MC check (independent seed): given $R\approx0.9$, observed midrange spread $\pm 0.0524 \approx \pm(1-r)/2$; $P(|M|\le 0.25 \mid R\approx 0.9) = 1.000$; $P(|M|\le 0.25 \mid R\approx 0.2) = 0.623$ vs theory $0.625$. Both candidate procedures' conditional coverage rises to certainty with $r$, so the planned contrast collapses — the exercise idea was indeed unsound.

**Replacement (Ex 06.5, equal-tailed vs HPD) checked and accepted.** Independent recomputation: ET$_{90}$ = [0.3554, 4.7439] (length 4.3885), HPD = [0.0838, 3.9321] (length 3.8483, content 0.9000, endpoint densities equal at 0.0771 — genuine HPD), 12.3% shorter ("about 12%" ✓). ET does exclude higher-density points than it includes: excluded θ=0.15 has density 0.1291 > 0.0413 at the included upper endpoint. All printed numbers match. The replacement also lands squarely on the module spine (a set is a decision).
