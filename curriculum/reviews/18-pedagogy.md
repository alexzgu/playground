## Learner's-eye summary (5 lines)

I came in thinking the top hit of a screen is my surest finding and that a measured 5.3 means an effect near 5.3; the module caught me on both — the tip is the *least* trustworthy, and shrinkage I get for free from modeling 1000 effects jointly fixes it. The M-open section genuinely scared me: a posterior can be flawlessly coherent and confidently wrong about its own width, and only an out-of-model audit (the sandwich) tells me. The four topics held together as "what your model's certainty is worth at scale," except the jump into misspecification felt like turning to a new chapter. The one place I wasn't *caught* being wrong was BH-vs-local-fdr — I was told they agree before I got to bet they wouldn't, so that lesson landed softer than the rest.
