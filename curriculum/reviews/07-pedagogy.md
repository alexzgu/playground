## What passes at the highest bar (recorded so revision doesn't break it)

- **Audit 1 — the lead.** §07.1 is the first content section and answers "does the prior even matter?" *before* any theory (Jeffreys/improper/nonidentifiability all follow). Fully staged with a captured two-number prediction (spread at n=10 vs n=10,000), the naive intuition named ("prior is a bias term, constant shift"), and a Reconcile that ties the miss to module 05's pseudo-count weight `(a+b)/(a+b+n)`. This is exemplary and the model the other demos should copy.
- **Audit 2 — spine.** Header spine matches the syllabus; the two-clause form ("influence decays 1/n for identified params, never for unidentified") is stateable a week later and recurs verbatim in §07.1, §07.6, Pitfalls, and Takeaways. The "part of the model, same status as the likelihood" framing is threaded through opener → Pitfall 5 → Takeaway 1.
- **Audit 3 — marquee captures.** Fan ✓ and washout-exception ✓ are genuine (commit-a-number, get-caught). Flat-isn't-flat and Haldane are the two gaps (findings above).
- **Audit 4 — non-polemical regularization.** §07.7 nails the panel's softened tone in one sentence: "not a claim that deep learning is secretly Bayesian, only that the regularization dial and the prior-variance dial are the same dial." "implicit" is in the heading; the payoff ("weakly-informative defaults as stability engineering") is concrete, not evangelical. Best-in-wave handling of this landmine.
- **Audit 5 — faces of nonidentifiability.** Lands as a *reusable model*, not a list: each of the three faces (structural / symmetry / weak-likelihood) carries a mechanism and a specific future encounter (M19 label-switching, M25 overparameterized nets, §07.1 small-n), closing with the unifying line "not a pathology to avoid; a fact to model, and the prior is how you model it." The learner leaves with a diagnostic, not three words.
- **Audit 6 — fluff/figures/exercises.** Fluff essentially clean (only the line-10 framing, sev-3). All 6 figures discussed with numbers. Exercises: 4 in exact §5 format; 07.3 genuinely surprising (three "noninformative" priors → 0.75/0.83/1.00 on identical data), 07.1 surprising at the boundary (Haldane improper), 07.2 is the ML bridge (weight-decay ↔ prior variance); every Reconcile explains WHY and ends on a compression line.
- **Audit 8 — length/voice.** 3,761 words, in range. Dense, dry, direct-address, no banned openers. Reads as narrative, not notebook — every section is a Setup→payoff arc — with §07.4 the sole drag (addressed by the block-merge finding).
