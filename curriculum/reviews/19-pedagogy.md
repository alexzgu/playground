## Learner's-eye summary (5 lines)
I ran k-means and GMMs for years and read `.predict()` labels as answers; this module made me see each label as a coin the posterior is still flipping. The EM-vs-Gibbs uncertainty map is the moment it clicked — EM's knife-edge boundary is one guess at a line whose *location* is itself unknown, and Gibbs smears it, flagging half again as many honest maybes. Label switching stopped looking like a sampler bug and started looking like a symmetry I'm supposed to quotient. Tempering finally showed me why my "stable" chains were lying, and the digamma correction taught me that α log n was quietly undercounting my clusters all along. The DP capstone reframed "how many clusters?" as a posterior I read rather than a K I pick — the single most useful re-wiring in the module.
