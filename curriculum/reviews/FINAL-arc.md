## State of the course (graded against BRIEF.md)

**The spine recurs and builds — this is the course's triumph.** The "Which line?" device appears in all 27 modules and is never mechanical: it ranges from "Line 2, mechanized" (10) to "upstream of line 1" (01), "a prequel to line 1" (24), "all four, viewed from outside" (08, the audit), "all four, in retrospect" (26). It is a genuine load-bearing coordinate system, not a header ritual. The Takeaways accumulate rather than fragment: module 05's master shrinkage formula is re-derived as Kalman gain (21) and partial pooling (16); module 03's KL is reused as training loss (15), variational objective (13), and predictive score (17), each cross-referenced; module 02's discarded denominator returns as evidence (17). By module 26 the six-row ledger and the closing paragraph ("you began able to *run* MLE, ridge, cross-entropy, dropout, an A/B test; you can now say what each one *is*") are the earned sum of what the 26 prior Takeaways actually taught — verified, not aspirational.

**Non-tribalism is airtight, and it is the course's spine as much as the four lines are.** A grep for partisan language ("frequentists are wrong/confused," "obviously better") returns nothing. Where the paradigms genuinely split, the course stages the disagreement concretely and refuses to pick a winner: the likelihood principle governs inference but not design (04/23); a p-value and a Bayes factor that disagree "answer different questions" rather than one "lying" (17); optional stopping is safe for the posterior but inflates Type-I error, and *selective reporting breaks both paradigms together* (23). Module 08's tone is set explicitly — "the mood is not gotcha; their crown jewel is our shrinkage" — and 26 closes on "calibrated Bayes: Bayesian for inference, frequentist for evaluation — run both." The course ends precisely as "one calculus, two audits," never drifting.

**The compression-of-years judgment nuggets are all present and are the intended ones.** The BRIEF's ten — Stein/inadmissibility (08), Lindley (17), stopping rules (23/04), the high-d donut/typical set (12), Occam-for-free-from-integration (17), ESS-not-draw-count (09/10), winner's curse (18), the collider trap (24), calibration (01/15/25), and marginalize-don't-profile nuisances (26) — are each not merely mentioned but promoted to a headline Takeaway with a printed number behind it. The panel's intended payload is delivered.

**Entry and exit doors both function.** Module 00 (Prereqs: None) is dense — PPV, ridge≡posterior-mean, staged decision losses, and a betting example in one module — but every term is defined in place and every claim is run, so the stated learner (CS + applied math, skimmed Casella–Berger) can start cold. Module 26's capabilities paragraph is honest: every method it claims the learner can now *place* (including dropout) is genuinely taught (MC-dropout is labeled "Heuristic" in 25.6), and its "sort an unseen method into theorem/approx/heuristic/none" promise is the drilled skill of 25's triage ledger.

**Bottom line:** a coherent, non-partisan, spine-driven whole that reads as one subject — the BRIEF's central ambition — with a single reader-visible seam (the calibration back-link) and two cosmetic polish items, none of which block shipping. Fix the 01→15 calibration clause before release; the rest can ship as-is or be swept in the paused consistency pass.
