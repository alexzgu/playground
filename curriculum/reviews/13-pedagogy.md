## Learner's-eye summary (5 lines)

I walked in thinking Laplace, EM, and VI were three separate tricks; I walked out with one identity — log p(x) = ELBO + KL-to-the-posterior — and three ways to spend the KL, which is exactly the compression the brief promises.
The CAVI section caught me clean: I was sure a factorized q would at least recover the marginals, and watching the variance collapse to *exactly* 1−ρ² (not "about") rewired that intuition permanently.
The EM box finally told me *why* EM has no error bars — a Dirac q(θ) with an exact q(z) — instead of asking me to memorize that it doesn't.
Exercise 13.1 was the sharpest hour: reverse-KL doesn't just narrow a multimodal posterior, it silently *deletes a mode*, and can hand me a different answer per random seed.
One thing nagged: I was told all three "maximize the ELBO," but §13.2 clearly just curvature-matches Laplace and grades it by KL — a sentence naming that seam would have kept the unifying story airtight rather than slightly too clean.
