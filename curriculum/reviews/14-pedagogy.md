# Pedagogy review — modules/14-bayesian-regression.md

**Verdict: REVISE** (2 major, 5 medium, 6 low). The content is excellent — the compression claims are real, the numbers contract holds, callbacks all check out against source logs. The defects are almost entirely *staging*: in all three marquee demos the explanation leaks ahead of the Predict beat, and in the module's judgment-compression peak (§14.4) the answer is literally stated two sentences before the reader is asked to commit.

## Audit answers (per brief)

1. **Trumpet (§14.2).** The Predict beat exists and demands a committed multiple ("same / 2× / far more" at x*=12), and the epistemic/aleatoric decomposition is printed concretely both on-support (`0.067` vs `1.000` at x=2) and off (`3.455` vs `1.000` at x=12) — that part is exemplary. But the beat is not genuinely predict-*first* as the syllabus requires: the variance decomposition and "grows without bound" appear *above* the Predict, giving away direction and leaving only magnitude at stake. The sklearn-mute contrast is asserted (a dashed line "no width") rather than experienced — the reader never calls `ols.predict([[12.]])` and receives a bare, unqualifiable number. Visually the flare reads modestly: on a slope-2 line running to y≈27, a band going from half-width 2.0 to 4.1 is not a trumpet bell, and the "data support" label collides with the legend.
2. **CV≈EB (§14.4).** Honest about tracking strength in prose (corr `0.795` quoted; "mild scatter" caveat with module 08/16 pointers) — good. But (a) "They are the same number." appears *before* the Predict beat, killing the commitment; (b) the headline is an identity claim warranted by a corr-0.795 empirical demo with no **Empirical** label and no statement of the actual identity (the prequential decomposition log p(y) = Σᵢ log p(yᵢ|y₁..ᵢ₋₁), which makes "the evidence is the leave-none-out predictive" precise) — the Reason paragraph gestures at it but never displays it; (c) EB is quietly handed the true σ²=1 while CV needs none.
3. **λ↔τ² (§14.3).** Lands as identity, unambiguously. Boxed α=σ²/τ², machine-precision table across two decades (worst `8.9e-16`), explicit "This is not an analogy," the σ²-cancels observation, and load-bearing callbacks to module 00's opener and module 06's MAP framing. No pedagogy defect. Best section of the module.
4. **t-robustness (§14.6).** The displacement works: scale-mixture identity derived, EM weights printed (`0.062 0.044 0.043`), "robustness = marginalizing a latent variance" bolded, and Exercise 14.3's redescending-influence surprise seals it. Two blemishes: the opener quantifies "a hundred times the leverage" before asking the reader to guess "~10%," so the 84%-vs-10% surprise is partially pre-spoiled; and "posterior weight E[λᵢ|data]" is loose — EM's weights condition on the fitted point estimate (β̂, σ̂²), not a posterior over β.
5. **Figure-before-algebra (§14.1).** No — the completing-the-square posterior (Σₙ, μₙ) precedes the posterior-over-lines figure. Worse, the section title promises "a *prior* over lines, conditioned into a posterior over lines," and the prior over lines is never drawn: only 30 posterior draws appear. The single most persuasive picture available — a wild prior fan collapsing into a pinched posterior fan — is left on the table.
6. **Skeleton/fluff/figures/exercises.** Skeleton fully compliant (header block, Bridge, 5 Pitfalls, 4 exercises in exact §5 format, 7 Takeaways). Fluff scan: clean — the opener ("OLS hands you *one* line. That is the tell…") is a model of §1 voice; no throat-clearing found. All 5 figures referenced and discussed. Exercises: 14.3 genuinely surprising (t-fit gets *more* resistant as Δ grows); 14.4 quietly the best (aleatoric floor vs epistemic shrink); 14.2's weight-decay bridge lands (0.1997 ratio vs "likelihood dominates" intuition, module 25 pointer); 14.1's "(surprising)" tag is half-earned — the far-field answer (width doubles as x doubles) *matches* the naive linear guess; only the near-field sub-linearity surprises.
7. **Mechanics.** 4,044 prose words (in range), 9 exposition + 4 exercise blocks (compliant), runtime ~12 s claimed, log confirms determinism of quoted numbers. All in-module backticked numbers ⊆ printed output. Cross-module callback numbers (`4.5e-15`, `0.1432`, `0.3945`) verified against modules 00/06 logs but are not printed by *this* module — a mechanical numbers-contract grep will flag them.

## Learner's-eye summary

As someone who has tuned `Ridge(alpha=...)` by `RidgeCV` a hundred times, §14.3 rewired me: the machine-precision table makes "the penalty is a prior precision" a fact I checked, not a slogan, and §14.7's "how many features → how strong a prior" reframe is the kind of compression the brief promises. But the module kept answering its own questions before I could bet: by the time it asked me to sketch the trumpet's width I'd already been told the epistemic term grows without bound, and §14.4 told me CV and EB are "the same number" two sentences before asking whether they'd track — so when corr 0.795 printed, I was never caught, only informed. The robust-t section genuinely displaced my "clip the outliers" habit — the printed weights 0.062/0.044/0.043 and Exercise 14.3's redescending surprise did it — though the opener spoiled the 84%-vs-10% shock. Give me the prior fan of lines before the algebra and hold the reveals, and this is an exemplary module.
