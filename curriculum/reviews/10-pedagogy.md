## What passes at the highest bar (recorded so revision doesn't break it)

- **Spine + the designated nugget.** "report ESS, not draw count" is not a slogan here — it is a bolded instruction backed by a computed 2.6× precision penalty (§10.5), a Pitfall, and a Takeaway, and the whole module is organized so §10.3 earns *why* ESS (efficiency, not acceptance) and §10.5 earns *how much* (efficiency 0.14). Necessary-not-sufficient is the through-line from Spine → §10.4 → §10.6 → Pitfalls → Takeaways.
- **Four genuine predicts.** §10.3 names two naive anchors (90% then 50%) and catches the reader at 0.234 with a flat-optimum nuance. §10.4 catches "ergodic + 40k steps ⇒ finds both modes" with `0` crossings and `P(θ>0)=0.0000`. §10.6 catches "single-chain R̂≈1 + few-hundred ESS ⇒ converged." §10.7 catches "decorrelates in a step or two." Each is a committed number/direction, not a rhetorical question — the STYLE §1 bar the sibling module 08 missed.
- **The fooling example is the payload.** It genuinely disturbs trust: every from-one-chain diagnostic certifies a chain that sampled nothing, and the reader is shown *why* (within-basin stationarity is real), then rescued by the multi-chain discipline the field converged on. Closing moral is verbatim-correct.
- **Theory held to "just enough."** DB proof is six checkable lines with the "sufficient not necessary" clause *demonstrated* (the 3-cycle, `max|flow−flowᵀ|=0.333`), not asserted. Spectral gap is one 2-state example whose measured ACF `[0.8023, 0.6447, 0.518]` lands on `λ₂^k = [0.8, 0.64, 0.512]`, tying ACF/ESS/R̂ back to 1−λ₂ and back-explaining §10.4's trap ("the thin bridge is a near-eigenvector at λ₂≈1"). Continuous operator cited by concept. No drift toward coupling/conductance/mixing-time lecture.
- **0.234 handled honestly.** Labeled **Theorem** (Roberts–Gelman–Gilks, asymptotic), scope words called "load-bearing," 1-D 0.44 and HMC 0.651 printed for contrast, flat-band caveat, and "design target you aim near, not a convergence test you must pass." No folklore.
- **Exercises carry judgment-compression.** 10.2 (surprising, marked): the higher-acceptance sampler *loses* by 10× ESS — "the waste that matters is not rejection; it is failure to move," with the SGD learning-rate parallel. 10.3: super-exponential barrier collapse (crossings 6650→2050→220), tied to the spectral gap. 10.4 (ML/DL bridge): energy-based models / partition function / contrastive divergence, the strongest ML tie. §5's surprising-and-ML-connected minimums both met.
- **Non-tribalism / voice.** No paradigm posturing (topic is computational); diagnostics framed as neutral audit tools. Opening is a M09 callback, not throat-clearing. Dense, no motivational padding.

## Learner's-eye summary (5 lines)

I came in trusting a clean trace; I leave knowing a clean trace is *cheap* and a single chain can lie to me twice — by never leaving one mode, and worse, by passing every audit I can run on it alone. The 0.234 rule stopped being a magic number: I watched ESS peak at 24% acceptance and saw the "accept everything" chain deliver half the ESS, so I now tune toward a band and report ESS, not draws. The fooling example is the one I'll remember — split-R̂ 1.00 on a chain that sampled nothing, then 1.74 the instant four chains disagreed — and it made "necessary, not sufficient" a reflex, not a phrase. The spectral-gap section quietly explained *why* everything else happened: mixing is 1−λ₂, and multimodality drives it to zero. My only friction was the two-state predict feeling pre-answered (the gap was handed to me before I guessed), and Exercise 10.1 landing the same "the normalizer cancels" punch that 10.4 lands harder — otherwise every page made me commit before it corrected me.
