## What passes at the highest bar (recorded so revision doesn't break it)

- **The arc.** Belief-first opening (line 10) is exemplary: every clause of "the MLE is obviously optimal" stated as a theorem, then "the conclusion is false the moment you estimate three means at once." §08.1 is titled "everything the MLE gets right" — it is belief-*reinforcement*, not neutral machinery, so it sharpens rather than delays the S4 shock. This is the correct reading of "spring S4 before the machinery": the only machinery ahead of it is the machinery that makes the belief airtight.
- **S4 staging (§08.2).** Committed prediction ("obviously not; borrowing strength can only inject noise") captured *before* any risk number ✓. Three genuinely unrelated quantities (batting/wheat/MPG) ✓. `9.98 → 1.99 → 1.25` delivers the fivefold gash; dominance sweep shows JS⁺ strictly under the flat MLE risk everywhere. The d=1,2 collapse is used to *sharpen* — "d−2 is the tell," crossover exactly at d=3, "three is the smallest number of unrelated problems you can couple" — not to dilute.
- **The punchline (§08.3).** JS = empirical Bayes derived cleanly (E[1/χ²_d]=1/(d−2) ⇒ (d−2)/‖X‖² is unbiased for B), verified numerically, and closed with the mandated sentence verbatim: "**admissibility forced frequentists to invent a posterior mean.**" The keystone lands.
- **BvM as reconciliation (§08.4).** Framed as the *answer* to "why did they agree in §08.1 and disagree in §08.2," not as a new topic. Conditions box is memorizable; the breakdown gallery (boundary / Neyman–Scott / nonidentified / misspecification) maps each failure to a numbered condition — a diagnostic, not a list. The M04 callback ("this is the deep reason module 04's Fisher-normal interval matched the exact Beta interval") is load-bearing.
- **Compactness of demoted results.** Cramér–Rao is a two-paragraph subsection inside §08.1 with one numerical check and the biased-estimator escape hatch that sets up Stein — exactly the supporting weight the panel wanted. Complete-class (§08.5) is one section framed as "'special case' is itself a theorem," not a standalone audit — it earns its length as the theorem that makes the reconciliation rigorous.
- **Exercises.** Three in exact §5 format. 08.1 (minimax coin) is genuinely surprising — flat risk `0.0144` at every θ, the "all reasonable estimators have parabolic risk" reflex caught. 08.2 is the d=1,2,5 surprise (perfect target still needs d≥3). 08.3 is the ML/DL bridge and the subtlest of the three: the misspecified-Laplace interval *still* covers at `0.946`, teaching that misspecification breaks coverage only when Fisher info misstates the sampling variance — honest, non-glib, ties to M18's sandwich and to OOD-overconfident nets. Three is the §2 minimum, but these three carry real judgment-compression; not a count defect.
- **Non-tribalism.** "not two warring creeds, but one calculus and two ledgers that agree wherever the conditions of §08.4 hold" (line 347) is the thesis, and it is earned by the coverage number, not asserted. Pitfalls are genuine practitioner traps (JS hurts an individual coordinate; credible≠confidence when a condition fails; CRLB doesn't bind biased rules; bootstrap understates tail uncertainty).

## Learner's-eye summary (5 lines)

I walked in sure the sample mean was unbeatable, and §08.1 made me *more* sure — then §08.2 asked me to bet that my baseball number couldn't possibly improve my wheat estimate, and I lost the bet 10-to-2. §08.3 then showed the estimator that beat me was just a posterior mean with its prior read off my own data, which reorganized everything: "frequentist" and "Bayesian" stopped being teams. BvM told me *why* they'd agreed all along and, honestly, exactly when they won't — I can now name the four conditions and the four ways they break. The one thing that dulled the jolt: a roadmap paragraph told me the MLE was going to lose before it asked me to bet it wouldn't, and a couple of the later "watch this" moments (especially σ̂²→σ²/2) would have caught me harder if they'd made me guess first. Fix those and the reveal is airtight.
